Falcon 160/185 Assembly Instructions.

Choose the right side of the PDB for your ESC polarity

When looking at the top of a Littlebee with the power wires away from you, you'll see the VIN is on the left.

When looking at the top of a KISS ESC with the power wires away from you, you'll see the VIN is on the right

The Flyno Cloverleaf PDB has two sides -- one of them has the VIN on the left, and one has the VIN on the right. Choose and mark the right side of the PDB before you begin.

Measure and cut pigtail wires

A good length for pigtail wires is 35mm.

Strip a 7mm section of wire off each side of your pigtail wires and twist the strands.

Solder pigtail wires to the XT60 connector

We recommend using 14 AWG wire for your pigtail.

Your soldering iron should be set to 750°F.

First, tin the poles on the XT60 connector. Apply some solder to your iron, insert it into the pole, and add a small amount of solder; just enough to make the inside of the pole silver.

Next, insert the wire into the pole, apply the tip of the iron to both the pole and the wire, and add a generous amount of solder. You should end up with a nice fillet, but it shouldn't overflow out of the pole.

Zip tie pigtail to the PDB

First, double check the side of the PDB you'll be using given your ESC polarity.

Next, find a small zip tie with a head that is less than 5mm in diameter.

Double check polarity (red lines up with VIN, black lines up with GND), then insert the wires into the VIN and GND holes on the PDB.

With the head of the ziptie under the PDB, insert it and loop it around, holding the pigtail into place.

Solder pigtail to the PDB

Your soldering iron should be set to 750°F.

With the pigtail in place, apply heat to the wire and the pad and add a generous amount of solder, enough to get a nice fillet. Make sure you've applied solder to all sides of the joint and that there are no voids.

Use your flush cutters to cut the resulting solder joint close to the PDB.

Solder VBAT wire to the PDB

Your soldering iron should be set to 720°F.

Solder a 2cm set of wires to the small VIN/GN pads to power your flight controller and/or provide battery voltage for telemetry purposes.

Optional: Solder 5V wires to the PDB

If your FC has an onboard 5V regulator (Dodo, Lux, etc) skip this step.

Solder a 2cm set of wires to the V(1) pads on the PDC.

Solder VTX power wires to the PDB

First, choose whether you're powering your VTX from a voltage regulator or directly from VIN.

Measure the distance you think you'll need to reach your VTX, add 1cm, and cut the wires.

Solder the wires to the correct pads (either VIN or V(2)).

Optional: Solder Pololu voltage regulators to the PDB

If your build uses voltage regulators, solder them to the PDB.

This guide assumes you'll install your 5V regulator to V(1) and your 12V regulator to V(2).

Pay attention to the pinouts, make sure VIN and VOUT line up.

Do not use the plastic portion of the pin header, simply solder the pins directly.

The voltage regulator should be flush to the PDB (not lifted off a few mm).

Optional: Solder any other wires you'll need on your PDB

Solder wires for your lap timing system, LEDs, etc at this time.

Install your battery strap

Taking care to orient the zip tie cutouts to the front/back of the frame, insert your battery strap and make sure you have the right side pointing out.

Your battery strap should weave through the frame, with a portion of it on the top of the frame.

Install the PDB

Holding the frame sideways, insert the 18mm screws into the frame.

Use some tape to hold the screws into the frame from underneath so they don't fall out.

Slide the 0.5mm insulating spacers onto the screws.

Slide the PDB onto the screws, taking care to orient the pigtail to the rear of the frame.

Wire up the FC

Solder the VBAT wires to the FC.

If necessary, solder the 5V wires to the FC.

Ensure the lenghts of your wires allow you to pick up and tilt your FC in the direction of the ESC headers such that you can access the bottom of it. Short wires are clean, but they make your build unmaintainable.

Solder motors to ESCs

If possible without exposing the coil wires, cut your motor wires to 2cm

Solder them straight to your ESCs.

Mount your motor + ESC combo

Get your Blue Loctite out and mount your motors to the arms.

For Cobra 2204/2300kV motors and an 4mm bottom plate, 6mm screws work.

Be sure to apply Loctite before mounting the motor, and take care to align the center of the shaft to the center of the cutout.

Now that your ESCs are on the arms, you may want to secure them with electrical tape to keep them in place while you build.

Measure and cut your ESC power wires

Eyeball the length of your ESC power wires. They should reach the end of the pad on the PDB.

Cut the wires and strip a 5mm section.

Slide heatshink onto your ESCs

Cut 4 3cm lengths of 9.5mm diameter (3/8") heatshrink.

Slide them onto the ESCs.

Solder your ESC power wires

Set your soldering iron set to 730°F.

Tin the ESC power wires generously. Don't leave a glob of solder, but leave a fair amount.

Tin the ESC pads, applying only enough solder to cover the entire pad and create a very small mound.

Tin your iron, grab the ESC power wire with your tweezers, apply it to the pad, then apply the iron on top of the ESC power wire and push it into the pad. Remove the iron, keeping the wire in place with your tweezers, and let the joint cool.

Measure and cut your ESC signal wires

Pick your flight controller up and tilt it in the direction of the ESC headers.

Measure them so they reach this headers with the FC tilted, add 5mm, then cut them.

Strip a 5mm section of wire.

Solder your ESC signal wires

Set your soldering iron set to 720°F.

Taking care to connect the correct ESC to the correct header, solder your signal wires.

  • Back right: 1
  • Front right: 2
  • Back left: 3
  • Front left: 4

Test and configure your ESCs

Using BLHeli Suite, configure and test your ESCs.

Motors 1 and 4 should spin clockwise, motors 2 and 3 should spin counter-clockwise.

Please refer to other guides for ESC programming instructions.

Wire up your RX

Measure and connect your depinned RX to your FC.

Use VHB double-sided tape to secure the RX to the FC.

Please refer to other guides for wiring instructions.

Install the FC

Add the 5mm unthreaded spacers between the PDB and FC, then screw the 15mm or 37mm threaded spacers onto the stack.

Your FC is now installed!

Test and configure your RX

Using Cleanflight configurator, test and configure your RX.

Please refer to other guides for RX setup.

Install the HD camera plate onto the top plate

Before adding the top plate to the stack, attach the HD camera plate to it.

Use 15mm standoffs in the rear and 20, 25, 30, or 37mm standoffs in the front (depending on your desired camera tilt angle).

Use 8mm screws on the bottom and 12mm screws on the top.

Apply loctite to the bottom 8mm screws (optional for the top 12mm screws).

Install your VTX to the top plate

In the low-profile build, you may secure your VTX to the top plate with a zip tie.

Mount the SMA pigtail

Mount the SMA pigtail to the top plate.

Configure your FPV camera

If you have a camera OSD configuration cable, configure your camera.

These settings are recommended.

Wire your FPV camera

If your VTX provides 5V power, wire that to your FPV camera.

Be sure to connect the video signal wire as well.

Install the top plate

Screw your top plate into place with the 8mm screws.

Connect camera and VTX

Connect the camera and VTX.

Zip tie and heatshrink the RX antennas

Using either the two slots in the rear of the top plate or the slot and SMA mounts on the left and right sides of the top plate, zip tie and heatshrink your RX antennas into plate.

Secure the ESCs

Using VHB double-sided tape, secure the ESCs to the arms.

You may also choose to use milities, zip ties, or electrical tape to keep them in place.

Go fly!

Assuming you've configured your FC and ESCs correctly, go maiden your freshly built Falcon!
